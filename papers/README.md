# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers (directly relevant to French counting in LLMs)

1. **Probing for Multilingual Numerical Understanding in Transformer-Based Language Models** (2010.06666)
   - Authors: Johnson, Mak, Barker, Loessberg-Zahl (2020)
   - File: `2010.06666_multilingual_numerical_probing.pdf`
   - Why relevant: **Most directly relevant** — probes BERT/DistilBERT/XLM on French, English, Danish, Japanese number understanding. French consistently worst-performing.

2. **Language Models Encode Numbers Using Digit Representations in Base 10** (2410.11781)
   - Authors: Levy, Geva (2024)
   - File: `2410.11781_digit_representations_base10.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Shows per-digit circular representations — key question is whether French word forms map to these representations

3. **Unravelling the Mechanisms of Manipulating Numbers in LMs** (2510.26285)
   - Authors: Štefánik et al. (2025)
   - File: `2510.26285_mechanisms_manipulating_numbers.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Universal sinusoidal representations — methodology for probing number internals

4. **LLMs Know More About Numbers than They Can Say** (2602.07812)
   - Authors: Yuchi, Du, Eisner (2025)
   - File: `2602.07812_llms_know_more_numbers.pdf`
   - Why relevant: Internal-vs-output gap for numbers — likely larger for French
